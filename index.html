QuantumBlack × Emirates Team New Zealand
Flying Across the Sea — AI & the America's Cup
Client Emirates Team New Zealand
Event 36th America's Cup
Domain AI, Deep Reinforcement Learning

QuantumBlack used deep reinforcement learning to teach an AI bot how to sail — and eight weeks in, the bot started beating professional sailors in the simulator. A thousand bots ran in parallel, learning from each other, creating the ideal way to test hydrofoil design variations around the clock. The result: New Zealand's design process accelerated by a factor of ten. They won their fourth title.

Deployed deep reinforcement learning to create an autonomous sailing simulator with 1,000 parallel bots for hydrofoil design optimization. Bots shared knowledge through a collaborative learning network, enabling 24/7 design iteration. After 8 weeks, the AI outperformed professional sailors in the simulator. Design iteration speed increased 10x. Emirates Team New Zealand won the 36th America's Cup.

QuantumBlack × Formula E
Breaking the Record — AI, Data & Electric Racing
Client Formula E / McLaren Racing
Event Guinness World Record Attempt
Domain GenAI, Real-time Analytics

QuantumBlack built the data-and-analytics platform behind a new Guinness World Record for indoor land speed — 218.71 km/h in a Formula E Gen3 car at ExCeL London. The driver interface analysed real-time telemetry to generate speed, power, and grip recommendations, allowing drivers Jake Hughes and Lucas di Grassi to fine-tune their approach across attempts. Built on Google Cloud's Vertex AI, the platform went from design to deployment in three weeks.

Built real-time data analytics platform for Guinness World Record indoor land speed attempt (218.71 km/h). Driver interface processed live telemetry — speed, power, grip — and generated actionable recommendations between runs. Built on Google Cloud Vertex AI. Design-to-deployment: 3 weeks. Drivers: Jake Hughes (McLaren) and Lucas di Grassi (Mahindra) in duel format at ExCeL London, 2023 Hankook London E-Prix.
