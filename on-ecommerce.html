How AI should work at every stage of On's e-commerce experience — from first click to lifelong customer. Not a funnel that extracts. A funnel that understands.

Scope Full e-commerce journey
Stages 6 AI-powered layers
Goal Relationship, not transaction
01 — The Full Funnel
Six stages, one intelligence

Most e-commerce funnels are a series of hand-offs: marketing acquires, the site converts, CRM retains. Each stage optimises for its own metric and no one owns the whole journey. On's AI Lab should change that. One model — the digital twin — threads through every stage, learning and adapting so the runner never feels a seam.

02 — Stage-by-Stage Deep Dive
What AI does at every moment

Each stage has its own intelligence layer, but they all feed the same model. The digital twin starts thin and grows richer with every interaction. Here's how each stage works — and what the runner actually experiences.

01
Discover
Getting them to On.com
AI-powered acquisition that speaks to who they are, not what they search for
Cohort-driven ad targeting
Five runner cohorts (Coffee, Monday, Studio, Trail, Elite) each receive different creative, messaging, and channel strategy. The Coffee Runner sees an editorial about morning ritual. The Elite sees race-day data.
AI-generated content at scale
Social, search, and editorial content generated per cohort and per moment. Not one campaign for everyone — hundreds of micro-narratives, each tuned to a specific runner's context.
Lookalike modelling from twin data
The digital twin's data feeds back into acquisition. Every converted runner makes the next prospect's targeting more precise. The model compounds.
Predictive intent from wearables
Partnerships with watch and insole data providers surface intent signals before the runner even searches. Mileage thresholds, injury patterns, seasonal shifts — the AI knows when to reach out.
02
Land
First 8 seconds on-site
Not a homepage. A mirror.
Personalised landing per cohort
The Coffee Runner sees lifestyle imagery and editorial tone. The Elite sees race performance data. Same URL, completely different experience. The page becomes a mirror of the visitor.
Dynamic hero content
Hero imagery and messaging adapt to referral source, time of day, weather, and location. A rainy-morning visitor in London sees different content than a sunny-afternoon visitor in LA.
Instant twin-seed
Before login, before cookies, the AI starts building a provisional profile from device type, location, browsing pattern, and referral context. The twin is born on first click.
"Not a homepage — a mirror"
The principle: every visitor should feel the site was built for them. Not personalisation-as-gimmick, but personalisation-as-architecture. The page structure itself adapts.
03
Browse
From looking to wanting
The longer you browse, the more the site becomes yours
Real-time recommendation engine
Not "people also bought" but "based on how you're moving through the site." The AI tracks dwell time, scroll depth, and click patterns to surface what matters to this specific runner, right now.
Visual search
Upload a photo of a running scenario — a muddy trail, a city street, a gym floor — and get matched products. The AI reads context, not just products.
The Fit Predictor
AI-driven size and fit recommendation from a phone camera foot scan. No more size uncertainty, no more returns. Confidence converts.
Smart bundling by cohort
Suggest apparel + shoe combinations based on the runner's cohort and browsing behaviour. Not random upsells — contextual kits that make sense for how they actually run.
Content-commerce fusion
Editorial stories that are shoppable, surfaced by AI based on reading patterns. The boundary between content and catalogue dissolves. An article about trail running becomes a shop of relevant gear.
Progressive personalisation
"The longer you browse, the more the site becomes yours." Every scroll, every hover, every tap feeds the model. Five minutes in, the site feels different than it did at first arrival.
04
Convert
Basket to checkout
The twin as checkout companion
AI-optimised checkout flow
Fewer steps for returning customers. The AI recognises intent velocity: a decisive buyer gets express checkout, a hesitant one gets reassurance and social proof.
Dynamic pricing signals
"This colourway is trending in your city" — social proof that feels local and relevant. Not manufactured urgency. Honest context.
Smart contextual upsell
The AI suggests the insole, the sock, the training plan — but only when it makes sense. Context, not cross-sell. The twin knows what complements this specific purchase.
Urgency without anxiety
"Your size ships tomorrow" not "ONLY 2 LEFT." On's checkout should feel like confidence, not pressure. Helpfulness converts better than fear.
One-click reorder
Returning customers can reorder their exact configuration in a single tap. The twin remembers size, colour preference, shipping address — everything.
Twin as checkout companion
"Based on your last pair, we recommend..." The digital twin surfaces insights from past purchases, wear data, and running patterns to make the checkout feel conversational.
05
Recover
When they don't convert
The whisper, not the shout
Intelligent abandonment analysis
AI determines WHY they left — price? size uncertainty? just browsing? — and tailors the follow-up accordingly. Not one recovery email for everyone. Hundreds of variants, each matched to the reason.
Personalised re-engagement timing
Not 1 hour later for everyone. AI learns when each person is most responsive. Morning person gets a Tuesday 7am nudge. Night owl gets a Thursday 9pm article. Timing is a feature.
Content-first recovery
Send them an article about their cohort's running style, not a discount code. The first touchpoint after abandonment should add value, not apply pressure. Earn the return visit.
Smart retargeting that fades
The whisper, not the shout. On's recovery should feel helpful, not desperate. Retargeting gets quieter over time, not louder. Respect builds trust. Trust converts.
Win-back through the twin
If they visited once and left, the provisional twin remembers. The next visit picks up where they left off — same products, same context, same progress. No starting over.
Solve, don't sell
If size uncertainty caused the abandonment, send the Fit Predictor. If price, wait for a natural moment (birthday, season change). Match the solution to the blocker, not the product to the person.
"On's recovery should feel like a friend who remembers what you were looking at — not a salesperson who followed you out of the store."
06
Retain
Post-purchase to lifetime
The twin as the retention engine
The Second Pair flow
AI tracks usage via insole and watch data and proactively suggests the next pair at the right moment. Not after a fixed time — after the shoe's actual performance curve indicates replacement. Precision, not cadence.
Continuous Pair subscription
The subscription that adapts. Each new pair reflects the runner's evolving gait data, terrain preferences, and mileage patterns. The model improves with every pair. LTV target: 3x vs single purchase.
Community integration
Connect new buyers to local run clubs, OAC content, and The Field Notebook. Matched by cohort and location. The purchase is the beginning of a community relationship, not the end of a transaction.
Lifecycle messaging
Not "buy more" but "here's what your shoe has learned about you this month." Monthly insights that deepen the relationship and make the twin visible and valuable.
Atelier invitations
High-value customers get invited to the Living Atelier — the physical space where LightSpray creates custom pairs. Scarcity, craft, and exclusivity as the retention tier that money can't buy but loyalty earns.
Cohort-matched referral
"Your friend runs like you — here's what we'd recommend for them." Referral program powered by cohort matching. The twin helps the runner recruit other runners who'll love On for the same reasons.
"Every interaction feeds the model. Every model output feels more personal. The twin is the retention engine — not a loyalty card."

04 — The Design Principle
A funnel designed to understand
The organising principle
Most e-commerce funnels are designed to extract.
On's should be designed to understand.

Every click, every browse, every abandoned cart is a signal — not a failure. The AI's job is to turn those signals into a relationship the runner actually wants. Not conversion optimisation. Relationship architecture. Not funnel metrics. Trust metrics. The difference is everything: one builds a quarter, the other builds a decade.

This is a companion piece to the full AI Lab vision. The funnel is where strategy meets the runner.
