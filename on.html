01 · How I see On

A brand that already runs
on craft. AI should make it
feel inevitable.

On isn't a tech company trying to add craft. It's a craft company that has quietly built one of the most technically ambitious supply chains in sportswear — LightSpray, CloudTec, 32 robots spraying a shoe upper in three minutes. The story is already radical. The question is what AI changes about how runners, retail partners, and the wider community experience it.

I believe the AI Lab's role isn't to layer AI features onto the product roadmap. It's to protect On's most valuable asset — its sense of taste — while using AI to deepen the relationship between the brand and its community at every touchpoint: the run, the store, the website, the recovery, the next pair.

"The best AI products don't feel like AI. They feel like the brand, turned up. That's what the Lab should be aiming for — not innovation as novelty, but innovation as intimacy."

The thread · Hyperpersonalisation

Not a configuration.
A conversation.

Every other brand treats personalisation as something you do at purchase — pick a colour, stitch your name, go. That's not personalisation. That's gift-wrap. The real opportunity is to design a loop where the shoe and the runner are in continuous dialogue, and every pair is a rendering of what On has learned about that person since the last one.

"Hyperpersonalisation isn't a feature you ship once. It's a relationship you design for years."

01
Identity
A persistent digital twin — foot, gait, biomechanics, history. Not a profile. A living model.
02
Expression
Every pair is a rendering of that twin at a moment. No two runners get the same shoe. No two pairs from the same runner either.
03
Adaptation
Sensors in the shoe feed wear and gait data back to the twin. Over 200km, it knows you better than you know you.
04
Context
The same runner gets different shoes for different moments. Race-day, recovery, jet-lagged travel. Personal × contextual.

Scenario B

She's tweaked her Achilles. Her physio notes flow into her twin (with her permission). The next refresh reinforces the heel counter and drops the offset by 2mm. The shoe heals with her.
"The shoe as quiet collaborator."

A lineage · Fashion × Performance

The spray has
history.

On didn't invent the theatre of spraying clothes onto bodies — fashion did. The difference is that fashion has treated it as spectacle, a one-night show. On has the manufacturing technology to make it a daily, scalable act. That's a rare place to stand.

The sweet spot the Lab should design for isn't "AI for sportswear." It's the much rarer overlap where high fashion's obsession with craft as performance meets high performance's obsession with craft as advantage. On already lives there. The question is how visible we make it.

The opportunity
Lab Concept · 18 months out
The Continuous Pair.

You don't buy shoes anymore — you keep a pair. Every 200km, you return to an Atelier. The upper dissolves back into material. A new one is sprayed in three minutes, tuned to how you just ran. The midsole is refreshed on a longer cycle. The shoe never dies. It evolves.

What you own at year one is measurably different from what you bought at year zero — softer here, firmer there, shaped by your actual runs. Zero waste by design. Your shoe becomes a record of your running life.

What Rolls-Royce did to aircraft engines ("power by the hour"), On could do to footwear: the product as an ongoing relationship, not a transaction.

04 · The horizons

How I'd structure
the 18+ month bet.

The Lab's mandate is to look 18+ months ahead. That's both a gift and a trap — too far out and the work becomes abstract; too close and it collides with the core roadmap. Here's how I'd split the work across horizons.

0 – 6 months
Foundation
Build the Lab's operating system. Map the existing journey across product, digital, retail, and service. Identify the three most promising intersections where AI actually earns its place. Ship the first testable prototype.
6 – 12 months
Prove the loop
Run the flagship Atelier pilot. Start feeding Lab insight back into the core roadmap quarterly. Establish the Lab as an insight engine — not a tech showroom.
12 – 18 months
Scale what sticks
Promote validated prototypes into core product or retail pipelines. Kill what doesn't resonate. Publish the Lab's first public POV — a signal to the industry and to On's own community.
18+ months
The compounding effect
The Lab becomes a recognised voice inside and outside On. Not a department that produces AI — a way of working that other departments borrow.
